It can be shown that the languages of the classical propositional logic and predicate logic are both context-free.

Context-free grammars are also known as Type-2 grammars in the Chomsky hierarchy. The Chomsky hierarchy specifies Type-2 grammars as consisting only of production rules of the form Aâ†’Î³, where A is a non-terminal and Î³ is a string of terminals and non-terminals.

Remarks.

  1. 1.

    A context-free grammar can be represented by a pushdown automaton. The automaton serves both as an acceptor for the language (that is, it can decide whether or not any arbitrary sentence is in the language) and as a generator for the language (that is, it can generate any finite sentence in the language in finite time).

  2. 2.

    The syntaxes of most programming languages are context-free grammars (or very close to it). A very useful subset of context-free languages are regular languages.

  3. 3.

    A context-free grammar G is context-sensitive if none of its productions has the form Xâ†’Î», where Î» is the empty word.

  4. 4.

    The family of context-free languages has the following closure properties: union, concatenation, Kleene star, homomorphism, inverse homomorphism, reversal, and intersection with a regular language. However, it is not closed under intersection, set difference, and complementation.
